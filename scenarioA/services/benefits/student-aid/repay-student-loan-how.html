Provincial payments

If you live in Quebec, Nunavut or the Northwest Territories

You pay back your loan directly to your province or territory. Contact your provincial loan provider to arrange payments.

If you live in Alberta, Manitoba, Nova Scotia or Prince Edward Island

You have provincial and federal parts of your loan that need to be repaid separately.

Follow the instructions from your provincial loan provider to repay your loan. You can follow the instructions on this page for the federal part of your loan.

Monthly loan payments

Making your first monthly payment

Six months after you finish school, you will need to start making monthly loan payments. You should receive a repayment plan in the mail from National Student Loan Service Centre (NSLSC) before the six month period is finished.

If you wish to make changes to your payment date, fill out the form and return it. If you do not sign it and send it back, the NSLSC will automatically use the suggested payment plan.

Pre-authorized monthly payments

Pre-authorized monthly payments will be taken out of your account automatically. You can use the same bank account that your student loans were deposited into to make payments.

If you do not have an authorized bank account on your file, you can sign up for pre-authorized payments:

Change your monthly payment

Change the payment date

You can change the day of the month your payment is due:

  • Change Your Payment Due Date form

Fill out the form and mail it to:


National Student Loan Service Centre
P.O. Box 4030
Missisauga, ON
L5A 4M4

Change the payment amount

Increasing your monthly payment

The amount you pay over and above your minimum monthly payment is applied directly toward the principal amount of your loan. This decreases the total loan amount and the total interest you will have to pay.

  • Customize your monthly payment amount in your NSLSC account

Decreasing your monthly payment

You can ask to have the terms of your loan changed if you need to reduce your payments below the minimum amount.

Generally, you have up to 114 months (9.5 years) to repay your loan. With a Revision of Terms, you may request an extended amortization period of up to 174 months (14.5 years).

This will make your monthly payments smaller, however, you'll end up paying more interest in the end because it will take you longer to pay back your loan.

  • Contact the National Student Loans Service Center (NSLSC) to to find out if you can do a Revision of Terms

Lump sum payments

Have the NSLSC withdraw a one-time payment from the bank account on your file or listed on the form:

  • One-Time Payment form

Fill out the form and mail it to:


National Student Loan Service Centre
P.O. Box 4030
Missisauga, ON
L5A 4M4

Online lump sum payments with HigherEdPoints.com

Alternatively, if you have an account with HigherEdPoints.com, a third party program to use reward points on student loans, you can make lump-sum payments through their account.

Choose your interest rate

You can choose from two interest rates for your student loans: fixed and variable.

For Canada Student Loans issued on or after August 1, 1995:

  • the fixed interest rate is prime + 5%
  • the floating interest rate is prime + 2.5%

For Canada Student Loans issued before August 1, 1995:

  • the annual interest rate is published each year in the Canada Gazette, Part 1
